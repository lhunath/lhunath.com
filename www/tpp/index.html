Trans-Pacific Partnership

We're working hard on creating a new partnership between nations that will change the Internet forever.

The TPP is almost ready!
Have a read through the list below for a quick glance at the amazing ways in which we're going to change your Internet. When you're done, let us know you're with us with the Agree button below.

Here's how we're going to change your Internet:

We'll install digital locks on your computers

Everybody knows you're terrible with computers.
We're going to make it illegal to do anything with your computer that the companies don't like you doing.

God forbid you try to repair it or play a DVD you bought on a trip to Europe, even worse, try to cheat in a game by modifying a save game. We know computers and phones are always breaking, but better that than you try and get your local geek to tweak it up for you.

Digital locks will improve the economy greatly by allowing companies to legally install virusses on people's computers and sell them software that's intentionally broken while selling excessively priced "repairs".

Seize your computer, confiscate your domain or jail you

We're going to make it possible to seize or confiscate your domains and even your computers or other hardware if we can see some kind of a link between you and somebody who we think may perhaps be meddling with copyrighted pictures, ringtones, songs or movie clips.

That'll keep you from getting involved with meme makers, terrible YouTube video posters or horrible phone ringtones.

Turn your Internet provider against you

By making ISP's into an effective private digital police force, we'll make sure they keep a close eye on what you're doing online.

There's too many of you for the authorities or big media companies to keep a close eye on, so making ISPs invest in spying equipment and threatening them with legal action if they don't police you will help keep those 4chan, YouTube, reddit and imgur holigans in line.

It'll even be free for us, because the ISPs will be able to just pass the buck on via the Internet subscription price you pay.

Ban you from the Internet

When we introduce three-strike legislation, we'll be able to ban people from the Internet when some guy presses report on a post of yours three times.

We don't really want to involve judges or fair legal process in any of this since it will just slow things down.

Prohibit re-using songs and art until 70 years after their artist died

Just in case we were wrong and the author didn't actually die, we need to keep all of this copyrighted art away from the grubby hands of the public who only create distasteful pictures and videos or terrible remixes with it.

In the mean time, we'll let the rich studios cash in on selling the art of this dead artist at extra steep prices by giving them monopoly rights to it.

Tax you to implement all of this world-wide

This is going to be expensive to implement and enforce, so we're going to implement high fees and costs to all participating nations.

We figure they can just pass the buck along via income tax or something similar.

Write secret laws and prevent senators and MPs from getting involved

Politicians are constantly arguing and have a hard time making up their minds on whether to protect their voters or the companies that pay them. We're going to write these laws in secret and involve only the very rich, like Holywood bosses, oil company executives and maybe that Facebook guy.

We'll make it super-long and when the law is ready, we'll make sure it becomes active before any politician has had the chance to give it a good read. We'll also make sure politicians can't just take it away later.

Exclude fair use protections

Comedians, DJs and and collectors will have you think it's great to reuse art in their skits or make personal copies or ringtones from the songs and movies they bought.

This is naturally ludecrous, those rights should be exclusive to the big studios so they can improve the economy by charging you excessive fees for doing things with the media you already own.

We're excited about the TPP

And we don't want you to hear about it or talk about it. It's best if you just ignore us and let the people in control change things for you. You can trust that we know what's better for us, after all, that's how we got to be where we are.
